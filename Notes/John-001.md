# John 1

## Chapter Introduction
John 1 presents evidence that Jesus is the Messiah, the Son of God. The chapter reveals Jesus' divine identity as the eternal Word who became human, records John the Baptist's testimony identifying Him as God's chosen one, and shows how the first disciples recognized Jesus as the fulfillment of Old Testament prophecies about the Messiah.

## Outline
1. Jesus is the Eternal Word of God (1-18)
2. John the Baptist Testifies About the Messiah (19-28)
3. Jesus is the Lamb of God (29-34)
4. Disciples Recognize Jesus as the Messiah (35-51)

## Study Notes

**1 In the beginning was the Word**. Jesus existed before all creation, proving He is more than a human teacher or prophet (Genesis 1:1; Proverbs 8:22-23). **The Word was with God, and the Word was God**. Jesus shares the same divine nature as God the Father, establishing His credentials as the divine Messiah (Colossians 2:9; Philippians 2:6).

**3 All things came into being through Him**. The Messiah is the Creator of all things, demonstrating His divine power (Colossians 1:16; Hebrews 1:2).

**4 In Him was life, and the life was the Light of men**. The Messiah is the source of spiritual life, bringing light to those in darkness (John 8:12; 14:6).

**9 There was the true Light which, coming into the world, enlightens every man**. The Messiah came into the world to bring spiritual understanding to all people (John 8:12; 12:46).

**11 He came to His own, and those who were His own did not receive Him**. The Messiah came first to Israel, but many rejected Him despite the prophecies pointing to His coming (Isaiah 53:3; Matthew 21:42-43).

**12 As many as received Him, to them He gave the right to become children of God**. Those who believe in the Messiah receive the privilege of becoming God's children (Romans 8:15-17; Galatians 3:26).

**14 The Word became flesh, and dwelt among us**. The Messiah took on human nature to live among His people, fulfilling prophecies of God with us (Isaiah 7:14; Matthew 1:23). **We saw His glory, glory as of the only begotten from the Father, full of grace and truth**. The disciples witnessed the Messiah's divine glory revealed in His life (John 2:11; 2 Peter 1:16-17).

**17 The Law was given through Moses; grace and truth were realized through Jesus Christ**. The Messiah brought the full revelation of God's grace that the Law could only point toward (Romans 6:14; Hebrews 10:1).

**18 No one has seen God at any time; the only begotten God who is in the bosom of the Father, He has explained Him**. The Messiah alone can fully reveal God because He is God's Son (John 14:9; Colossians 1:15).

**23 I am a voice of one crying in the wilderness, 'Make straight the way of the Lord'**. John the Baptist fulfilled Isaiah's prophecy about the messenger preparing the way for the Messiah (Isaiah 40:3; Malachi 3:1).

**29 Behold, the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world**. John identified Jesus as the Messiah who would be the ultimate sacrifice for sin (Isaiah 53:7; 1 Peter 1:19).

**32 I have seen the Spirit descending as a dove out of heaven, and He remained upon Him**. God confirmed Jesus' identity as the Messiah by sending the Holy Spirit upon Him (Isaiah 11:2; Matthew 3:16).

**34 I myself have seen, and have testified that this is the Son of God**. John the Baptist gave eyewitness testimony that Jesus is the Messiah, the Son of God (Matthew 3:17; John 20:31).

**41 We have found the Messiah (which translated means Christ)**. Andrew immediately recognized Jesus as the promised Messiah, the Anointed One sent by God (Daniel 9:25-26; John 4:25).

**45 We have found Him of whom Moses in the Law and also the Prophets wroteâ€”Jesus of Nazareth**. Philip understood that Jesus fulfilled all the Old Testament prophecies about the coming Messiah (Deuteronomy 18:15; Luke 24:27).

**47 Behold, an Israelite indeed, in whom there is no deceit**. Jesus demonstrated supernatural knowledge, a sign of the Messiah (1 Samuel 16:7; John 2:25).

**49 Rabbi, You are the Son of God; You are the King of Israel**. Nathanael confessed Jesus as the Messiah after witnessing His divine knowledge (Psalm 2:6-7; Zephaniah 3:15).

**51 You will see the heavens opened and the angels of God ascending and descending on the Son of Man**. Jesus revealed that He is the connection between heaven and earth, fulfilling the role of Messiah as mediator (Genesis 28:12; 1 Timothy 2:5).
